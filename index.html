Introduction to Reponsive Web Design

Every day, millions of people are using the web. People access the web on different devices with different screen size. Ensuring perfect legibility, user friendly and attractive regardless of screen size has been one of the top requirement for every website. The major benefit to designing responsively is that you don't have to create multiple versions of a website, nor do you have to spend extra marketing dollars convincing audience to download a native app.

The Web Has Changed

Let's travel back to the time before the iphone, when people use flip phones, the Nokia or blackberry phones. The web is like the thing of your family to share (a desktop), maybe a personal laptop for few people who need it for works. At that time, the web has only static design and fixed size of web page for every computer screen. Before mobile and tablet are released, web is only for computer screens to display.

Today, millions of different devices with many different screen sizes are used everyday and everywhere. An evolving landscapem where new technology are pushing the boundaries of web experiences. SmartTV, Smart Watch, Mobile, Tablet, etc. are released and updated quickly, and the web need to evolve to adapt the changes of devices.

The Responsive Age

Websites, applications, software GUI are not just a static or fixed templates anymore. They are frames in an extended fluid experience.

The Fluid Concept

From Fig.02 above, water or fluid in general is the content that can fit in all different bottles. Let's imagine that the content of your website is the fluid, and the water bottle is the device's screen. Water or fluid is not fixed, it is responsive and adaptive. Similar to fluid and bottle, the content of the web is responsive when it fits in different devices adaptively and responsively.

The Definition

Common definiton: It looks different and things are changed around, scale, re-order, appear, and disappear so it works well in your browser's different size or whatever device you're using to view.

Formal definition: It means designing the website so that it responds to your users' environment based on screen-size, platform and orientation.

Ethan Marcotte

Ethan Marcotte is known as one of the first person who wrote article and book about Responsive Web Design. According to Ethan, responsive web design is not only about adjustable screen resolution and automatically resizable images, but rather about a whole new way of thinking about design. His thinking is through the concept of responsive architectural design, whereby a room or space automatically adjusts to the number and flow of people within it.

“ Rather than quarantining our content into disparate, device-specific experiences, we can use media queries to progressively enhance our work within different viewing contexts. ” - Ethan Marcotte
